Challenges 1 - Design for a comfortable experience

Originally, we considered designing the game with a fabulous UI style. However, considering the limitation to focus on fancy graphics, players cannot focus on the most important information, and the UI tends to be out of sight for the players to interact with.

Solution:
I aligned the issue with the team and we switched to diabetic UI with low transparency. Then, we focused on design for the standard line of sight. Yet we kept all the UI within the most comfort zone with the best sight sitting. We designed the experience so that the player can go through the journey in the most comfortable way, to practice mediation.

Challenges 2 - How to design for smooth transition

In the original design, the interaction with fish is either too fast or lack of realism. It will break the immersive experience by just pointing the fish and play with them. 
Previous action : Pointer hover – Short haptic → Laser changes color on hovered creature
Grab – Long haptic → Hold 

Solution: New interaction 
Action: Pointer hover – Short haptic → Highlight the fish and show UI card
Feed → Fish follow particles to smoothly move closer → Long haptic on grabbed
