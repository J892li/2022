Why we are doing this project? 

This project is under NDA, so I cannot share some of the content publicly. 

The VOTR project is the Canada’s first Métis mixed reality game combines traditional knowledge with new media, weaving storytelling, cultural practices, and language into an evolving gameplay with rich, cultural mechanics. Key features include:harvesting, crafting and combat  

First, the process 

This is a four-month project with the goal of providing our clients with a working Unity prototype. During those four months, our team completed 4 sprints, each consisted of 2 weeks. 

Timeline

Challenges 1 - How to avoid cultural misunderstandings in production

This project is Canada’s first Métis mixed-reality game that
combines traditional knowledge with new media.
How can we avoid the wrong usage of cultural elements in the design process?

Solution:

1. Our team carefully read the onscreen
protocol for the use of indigenous cultural
elements. While in production, we believe
respect, consent and mutual agreement is the
key to avoiding mistakes.

2. We communicate with the production team
frequently to understand the behind-the scenes
stories and background and to align
our game with the main story.

Demo 2 for Harvest

Challenge 2: How to design for a cohesive experience？

This project contains three main milestones, which are the three main systems in the game.
Yet, the challenge is, after we designed an individual system, how can we combine the selected ideas into one Unity prototype?

Solution:
We always  ask the team:   why we are doing this design？
We review our sprint results and testing results, iterate our previous sprint artifacts, then deliver the final solutions to our client.

 

Team Punch and kick 

Challenge 3: How to build a synergistic environment

The development process can be
frustrating.
Sometimes our artist/coders are heavily loaded in the middle of the
project, and our team is having conflicts about the design for the
game. The challenge is, for me, how to help the team perform better? 

Solution:
1. Team Transparency : Kanban
The Kanban board is a good tool for the team to
understand the ongoing situation regarding sprints and
help the team to remove blockers

2. Improvements : Sprint retrospective
The team will share their opinions about how to improve
current sprints and how to improve the next artifacts

3. Team environment : KEEP, FIX AND CHANGE
The team will also sit down with each other to talk about
the things we should keep, fix and change as a team, for
better collaboration

Summary



Things I will do differently：
Next time I might not split the game into separate pieces but design as a whole first. In this way maybe we can avoid the issue of thinking about holistic experience.  

Things I will keep：
 
Be agile in making plans
A mixed-reality game project is heavy in its technical aspect. The right estimation of tasks (If the tasks are too long, or too short), and the right adjustment of the project plan are significant
in the production process.

Build positive synergy around the team
I believe the passion of the team is one of the key factors for the completion of this game project.
While we are a design team trying to create a great experience, we also enjoyed the process of delivering our value, and our love for the story.
It is also the passion of the team that conveyed the story successfully to a digital product
